# QuickBooks for Education Feature Spec

## Overview
QuickBooks (by Intuit) is the world's most popular general accounting software. While not built specifically for schools, it is the default choice for thousands of small to mid-sized educational institutions due to its familiarity, cost, and vast ecosystem.

## Core Features
### General Accounting
*   **Chart of Accounts:** Highly customizable; schools typically set this up to mirror fund accounting (though it requires manual configuration).
*   **AP/AR:** Standard modules for managing vendor bills and customer (parent) invoices.
*   **Bank Feeds:** Automatic syncing with bank accounts for easy reconciliation.

### Education-Specific Use Cases
*   **Class/Tag Tracking:** Using "Classes" or "Tags" to track revenue and expenses by department, grade level, or specific program (e.g., Music Dept vs. Athletics).
*   **Invoicing:** Generating invoices for tuition, but usually lacks the "payment plan" automation found in dedicated school software.

### Reporting
*   **Standard Financials:** P&L, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow.
*   **Custom Reports:** Can be filtered by "Class" to provide departmental budgets.

### Payroll
*   **QuickBooks Payroll:** Integrated module for managing staff salaries, taxes, and direct deposit.

## Online vs. Desktop
*   **QuickBooks Online (QBO):**
    *   **Pros:** Cloud-access, automatic backups, better integrations, free for eligible educators/students.
    *   **Cons:** Monthly subscription, less powerful reporting than Desktop.
*   **QuickBooks Desktop (Enterprise):**
    *   **Pros:** Advanced reporting, industry-specific versions (though "Education" is not a native industry preset like "Nonprofit"), handles larger data sets better.
    *   **Cons:** Locally installed, higher upfront cost, manual backups.

## Pricing
*   **Intuit for Education:** Free QuickBooks Online access for eligible U.S. educators and students (1-year trials for students, ongoing for teachers).
*   **Standard Business Pricing:**
    *   **QBO Simple Start:** ~$30/mo.
    *   **QBO Plus (Recommended for Schools):** ~$90/mo (includes Class tracking).
    *   **Desktop Enterprise:** ~$1,830+/year.

## Integration Capabilities
*   **SIS Platforms:** Almost every major SIS (FACTS, PowerSchool, Blackbaud, SchoolCues) has a "QuickBooks Export" or direct sync tool.
*   **Zapier:** Can connect to thousands of other apps (e.g., Google Sheets, CRM tools).

## Pain Points
*   **Not "School-Aware":** Does not natively understand concepts like "Enrollment Status," "Financial Aid Awards," or "Sibling Discounts."
*   **Manual Entry:** Without a tight SIS integration, school offices spend significant time manually entrying data.
*   **Fund Accounting:** QBO is built for "for-profit" accounting; true fund accounting (tracking restricted vs. unrestricted funds) requires complex workarounds.

## Competitive Intelligence
*   **vs. Sage Intacct:** QuickBooks is the "entry-level" choice. Sage Intacct is where schools go when they outgrow QuickBooks' reporting and multi-entity limitations.
*   **Market Position:** Ubiquitous. Even schools using FACTS/Blackbaud for tuition often use QuickBooks as their "back-end" general ledger.
