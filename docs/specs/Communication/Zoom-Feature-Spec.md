# Zoom Feature Spec — Virtual Meetings

## Overview
Zoom for Education is a secure, high-performance video conferencing and collaboration platform designed for K-12 and higher education. It goes beyond simple video calls to provide specialized features for virtual classrooms, professional development, and community webinars.

## Core Features

### 1. Virtual Classroom & Meetings
*   **High-Capacity Meetings:** Host up to 300 participants per meeting (expandable to 1,000+).
*   **Breakout Rooms:** Create up to 100 separate virtual spaces for small group work, with manual or automatic assignment and teacher monitoring.
*   **Interactive Tools:** Built-in polling, non-verbal feedback (reactions), and chat functions.
*   **Waiting Rooms:** Secure control over who enters the meeting, ensuring privacy and safety.

### 2. Collaboration & Engagement
*   **Screen Sharing & Annotation:** Co-annotation on shared screens for real-time instruction.
*   **Zoom Whiteboard:** Persistent, infinite digital canvas for collaborative brainstorming and visual learning.
*   **AI Companion:** Generate meeting summaries, highlight key points, and create study guides.
*   **Recording & Transcription:** Cloud recording with automated, searchable transcripts and closed captioning.

### 3. Webinars & Community
*   **Zoom Webinars:** Large-scale events for school board meetings, virtual conferences, or community outreach (up to 1,000+ attendees).
*   **Webinar Features:** Q&A panel, practice sessions, and registration management.

### 4. Admin & Security
*   **Education-Specific Security:** Virtual backgrounds to protect student privacy, disciplinary controls (mute, remove, lock), and SSO/external authentication.
*   **Admin Dashboard:** Centralized management of users, licenses, and meeting analytics.
*   **Zoom Phone:** Cloud-based phone system for school-wide voice communication.

## Integrations
*   **LMS (LTI Pro):** Seamless integration with Canvas, Blackboard, Moodle, and Desire2Learn for scheduling and joining meetings directly from course pages.
*   **Business Tools:** Google/Outlook Calendar, Slack, and Microsoft Teams.
*   **Content:** Google Drive, Microsoft OneDrive, and Dropbox.

## Pricing (2024–2025)
*   **School & Campus:** ~$10.00/month/license (minimum 20 licenses).
*   **School & Campus Plus:** ~$15.00/month/license (minimum 20 licenses).
*   **Enterprise (Education):** Custom pricing for large institutions with additional features (e.g., higher participant limits).
*   **Add-ons:** Zoom Webinars (~$79/month+), Zoom Phone (~$8/month+), Zoom Rooms (~$49/month+).
*   **Free Option:** Basic student licenses for higher education and K-12 educators (limited features).

## Pain Points
*   **"Zoom Fatigue":** Over-reliance on virtual meetings can lead to student/staff burnout.
*   **Security Concerns:** Despite improvements, "Zoom-bombing" remains a concern if security settings (Waiting Rooms, Passcodes) aren't properly enforced.
*   **Breakout Room Recording:** Breakout room sessions are not included in the main cloud recording, which can make it difficult for teachers to review all group work later.

## Competitive Intelligence

### Zoom vs. Google Meet / Microsoft Teams
| Feature | Zoom | Google Meet / Teams |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Breakout Rooms** | Very Advanced (Manual/Auto) | Good (Improving) |
| **Webinars** | Enterprise-Grade Features | Solid (Bundled) |
| **Whiteboard** | Highly Collaborative/Persistent | Basic (Integrated) |
| **Ease of Use** | High (Dedicated Client) | Integrated (Web-First) |
| **Integrations** | Extensive (LTI Pro) | Ecosystem-Locked (Workspace/Office) |

*   **Key Advantage:** Zoom remains the gold standard for "virtual instruction" due to its robust breakout room management and specialized LTI integrations that connect it directly to school curricula.
